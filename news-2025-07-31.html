Tuesday, July 30, 2025
Orissa High Court Reviews Mahaprayan Scheme at SCBMCH
The Orissa High Court reviewed the dysfunctional status of the Mahaprayan scheme at SCB Medical College in Cuttack. Two vehicles meant to carry deceased bodies had broken down, forcing families to arrange private transport. The court directed timely repairs to ensure dignity in death services.
Mission Shakti Signs MoU to Empower Women Weavers
On July 29, Mission Shakti signed an MoU with the Weavers’ Service Centre to modernize Odisha’s weaving sector. Women-led SHGs will receive design training, branding, and digital marketing support, boosting the “Lakhpati Didis” initiative.
Odisha TEX 2025 Summit Attracts ₹7,800 Cr in Investments
The Odisha TEX 2025 summit concluded with 33 MoUs worth ₹7,808 crore. The state targets over 53,000 new jobs in textiles and apparel, aiming to become eastern India’s textile powerhouse.
Aspirational Market Campaign Launched by CM
Chief Minister Mohan Majhi launched a campaign to promote local products in remote districts. The initiative supports artisans, SHGs, and farmers, aligning with the national 'Vocal for Local' drive.
Odisha NEET Counselling: Provisional Merit List Released
The OJEE Committee released the provisional merit list for NEET UG counselling. Candidates have until 5 PM today, July 30, to raise objections. Final list to be published on August 1.
DRDO Successfully Tests 'Pralay' Missile off Odisha Coast
The DRDO conducted successful tests of the tactical ‘Pralay’ missile from Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam Island on July 28–29. The trials pave the way for induction into armed forces.
India’s Largest Air Marijuana Seizure at Bhubaneswar Airport
Authorities seized 25 kg of hydroponic marijuana at Bhubaneswar airport, worth ₹30 crore. Hidden in food packets, this is India’s largest-ever marijuana seizure by air.
Teacher Detained in OTET Paper Leak Case
Odisha Crime Branch detained a teacher from Rayagada in connection with the July 20 OTET paper leak. Several exam officials have been suspended pending further investigation.
Opposition Flags Rise in Women-Related Crimes
Opposition parties submitted a memorandum to the Governor over 18,000 crimes against women in 2025 with only 217 resolved. They demanded stronger legal action and reactivation of the State Women’s Commission.
Odisha to Become FMCG & Processed Food Export Hub
IPICOL MD Bhupendra Singh Poonia stated Odisha is set to lead FMCG and processed food exports in eastern India due to robust policies, low logistics cost, and proactive governance.
Pregnancy of 2 Minor Girls in Govt Hostel Sparks Uproar
Two Class 10 girls residing in a government hostel in Kandhamal were found pregnant. FIRs have been lodged, and the incident raised questions over safety in state-run hostels.
